Designing a Good Country Flag

Countries in Second Earth are based on real countries, and thus by default, have the same flag as their real-world counterparts. You may, however, choose to design your own flag.

You do not need to read a vexillogy book from cover to cover, but there are some guidelines you should follow to create a recognisable and clean-looking flag.

Keep your flag minimal

Your flag should be easy to recognise at small sizes, and so should not rely on intricate details. If your flag can not be roughly drawn or described from memory, you might need to rethink your design.

Remember your history

Good flags reflect the history of what they represent, and they seldom change majorly between interations. As such, if you're making a custom flag, you might want to take inspiration and design elements from the original flag so that it remains recognisable.

Think carefully about your colours

Take your time to think about the colours you use in your flag and their semantics. Here is a list of some of the most common colours, and their connotations:

  • Red: war, blood, courage
  • White: peace, purity
  • Blue: liberty, justice, patriotism
  • Yellow/Gold: wealth, glory, sunlight
  • Green: nature, peace, fertility
  • Black: darkness, chaos, anarchy

Purple and pink are almost never used in flags. In the past, the pigments of these colours were expensive and hard to come by, which is why purple is often used to represent royalty.

Most flags do not use more than two or three main colours, and seldom have more than five colours in total.

We also recommend picking the colours out of Discord's flag emojis to ensure that we can match them as closely as possible when we create the custom emojis.

Do not use text in your flag

Do not use any text in your flag. Some real-word flags do have small amounts of text, but it is hard to read and replicate when we create the custom flag emojis, so we recommend not including it.

Match other flags' ratios

Take a look at the size of other world flags. Most are rectangular and wider than they are tall. Make sure yours is rectangular and can fit into the footprint of other flags.

As cool as Nepal's flag is, it's very difficult to match with custom emojis, so we ask that all flags be rectangular.
